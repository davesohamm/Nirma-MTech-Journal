### 🌆 After the Exam

At 5 PM, I clicked a picture of the classroom for the last time.  
The same classroom walls, benches, screens, and silence that had seen endless lectures, submissions, stress, laughs, and dreams over these years. It felt strange walking out, knowing this was the final exam day of my academic life at Nirma.

And then... I saw *her*.  
Standing outside, waiting for me.

I immediately greeted her, and for a moment, everything else around me faded away.

We walked towards the parking area, talking softly, carrying the strange mix of relief and sadness that only endings bring. From there, we went to Gwalia canteen and had ice slush together. Later, we walked to the Law canteen and ate Alfredo pasta. Many of my batchmates probably saw *us* together properly for the first time — from a distance, obviously.

The evening slowly stretched into the night.

We had a small fight too. Tiny misunderstandings, emotional exhaustion, maybe the weight of the day itself. But we resolved it afterwards. We ate Faasos wraps, had cold drinks, and sat in the open-air lobby area of the college till around 11:30 PM.

Every passing minute felt heavier.

Saying goodbye to her became incredibly difficult.  
Maybe because this wasn’t just the end of an exam day.  
It was the end of an era.

From next week onwards, I will be moving to Gurgaon for my internship. Even though one academic year is technically left, it will mostly be internship semesters, so I probably won’t return to college life the same way again.

She became very emotional while saying goodbye today.

Inside my mind, there was an emotional whirlwind — memories, uncertainty, attachment, fear of distance, hope for the future — all flowing together at once. But in front of her, I tried to stay calm and composed.

At around 11:45 PM, we finally left Nirma.  
The university gates officially close at 11:30 PM, so the security guards stopped us while exiting. Somehow, we managed and eventually left for home.

---

### 💭 Reflections

So that was the last academic day of my M.Tech journey at Nirma.

It was truly a roller coaster ride — full of all kinds of experiences, people, lessons, failures, achievements, stress, growth, and unforgettable memories.

I will miss the college.  
I will miss the atmosphere.  
And I will miss *her* the most.

Reached home around 12:15 AM.
